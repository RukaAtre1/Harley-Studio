About me

Who I am, what I study, and what I am trying to build.

I am a UCLA student majoring in Statistics and Data Science and minoring in Data Science Engineering, with a growing focus on AI systems that have to work under real human and organizational constraints. I’m less interested in isolated model performance than in what happens after deployment: whether outputs are grounded, whether users can verify them, and whether the product deserves long-term trust.

My recent work has concentrated on educational AI, creative AI, and data-centric infrastructure. Across these domains, I keep returning to the same question: how do we turn fragmented signals into reliable decisions or useful interfaces?
